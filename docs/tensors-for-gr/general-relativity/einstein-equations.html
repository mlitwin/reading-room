The Einstein equations

Matthew Litwin 2026-05-21

Setup

Spacetime is a four-dimensional smooth manifold MMM equipped with a Lorentzian metric ggg of signature (−,+,+,+)(-, +, +, +)(−,+,+,+). Test particles follow timelike (massive) or null (massless) geodesics of the Levi-Civita connection of ggg. The geometry of ggg is the gravitational field; the dynamical content of GR is the equation that determines ggg from matter content.

The equations

The Einstein field equations are

  Gμν+Λ gμν=8πGc4 Tμν,  \boxed{\; G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda\, g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\, T_{\mu\nu}, \;} Gμν​+Λgμν​=c48πG​Tμν​,​

where:

  • Gμν=Rμν−12 R gμνG_{\mu\nu} = R_{\mu\nu} - \tfrac{1}{2}\, R\, g_{\mu\nu}Gμν​=Rμν​−21​Rgμν​ is the Einstein tensor built from the Ricci tensor and scalar curvature of ggg.
  • Λ\LambdaΛ is the cosmological constant — a scalar parameter; observationally non-zero and positive.
  • GGG is Newton’s gravitational constant and ccc the speed of light; 8πG/c4≈2.08×10−43 N−18\pi G / c^4 \approx 2.08 \times 10^{-43}\, \mathrm{N}^{-1}8πG/c4≈2.08×10−43N−1 in SI units. Most GR work sets c=1c = 1c=1 and G=1G = 1G=1, in which case the prefactor is 8π8\pi8π.
  • TμνT_{\mu\nu}Tμν​ is the stress–energy tensor of matter and non-gravitational fields. Symmetric, (0,2)(0, 2)(0,2)-tensor; its components encode energy density, momentum density, and stress.

Ten components on each side (ggg is symmetric, so GGG and TTT are too; in n=4n = 4n=4 that’s 101010 independent entries each). Modulo the four-fold gauge freedom of diffeomorphism, this is six independent equations for the six geometric degrees of freedom of gμνg_{\mu\nu}gμν​ in $4$D.

Why this combination?

The left-hand side is forced by three requirements:

  1. A symmetric (0,2)(0, 2)(0,2)-tensor. Same shape as TμνT_{\mu\nu}Tμν​.
  2. Built from ggg and at most its second derivatives. Lovelock’s theorem: in $4$D, the unique tensors meeting requirements 1 and 2 are linear combinations of RμνR_{\mu\nu}Rμν​, R gμνR\, g_{\mu\nu}Rgμν​, and gμνg_{\mu\nu}gμν​.
  3. Divergence-free. Local conservation of stress–energy ∇μTμν=0\nabla^\mu T_{\mu\nu} = 0∇μTμν​=0 requires the same on the left. The contracted Bianchi identity gives ∇μGμν=0\nabla^\mu G_{\mu\nu} = 0∇μGμν​=0 automatically; ∇μgμν=0\nabla^\mu g_{\mu\nu} = 0∇μgμν​=0 by metric compatibility. So Gμν+Λ gμνG_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda\, g_{\mu\nu}Gμν​+Λgμν​ has divergence zero for any Λ\LambdaΛ.

This is essentially the entire derivation, and explains why the Einstein equations are nearly inescapable once you ask for a tensorial second-order classical theory of a metric.

Variational form

The Einstein equations are the Euler–Lagrange equations of the Einstein–Hilbert action:

SEH[g]=c416πG∫M(R−2Λ) volg+Smatter[g,ψ],S_{\mathrm{EH}}[g] = \frac{c^4}{16\pi G} \int_M (R - 2\Lambda)\, \mathrm{vol}_g + S_{\mathrm{matter}}[g, \psi], SEH​[g]=16πGc4​∫M​(R−2Λ)volg​+Smatter​[g,ψ],

where ψ\psiψ collectively denotes matter fields. Varying SSS with respect to gμνg^{\mu\nu}gμν and integrating by parts yields the field equations with

Tμν=−2∣det⁡g∣ δ(∣det⁡g∣ Lmatter)δgμν.T_{\mu\nu} = -\frac{2}{\sqrt{|\det g|}}\, \frac{\delta (\sqrt{|\det g|}\, \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{matter}})}{\delta g^{\mu\nu}}. Tμν​=−∣detg∣​2​δgμνδ(∣detg∣​Lmatter​)​.

This is the “definition” of TμνT_{\mu\nu}Tμν​ in field-theoretic GR — the response of the matter Lagrangian to a perturbation of the metric. Stress–energy is what couples to gravity.

Vacuum and matter

Vacuum. With Tμν=0T_{\mu\nu} = 0Tμν​=0 and Λ=0\Lambda = 0Λ=0:

Gμν=0⟺Rμν=0.G_{\mu\nu} = 0 \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad R_{\mu\nu} = 0. Gμν​=0⟺Rμν​=0.

The trace of Gμν=0G_{\mu\nu} = 0Gμν​=0 gives −R=0-R = 0−R=0, so R=0R = 0R=0 and the equation collapses to Rμν=0R_{\mu\nu} = 0Rμν​=0. Vacuum solutions are Ricci-flat Lorentzian 444-manifolds. The Riemann tensor need not vanish — Weyl curvature can carry the gravitational degrees of freedom — and there are non-trivial solutions like Schwarzschild and gravitational waves.

With matter. A few stress–energy tensors that come up:

  • Perfect fluid: Tμν=(ρ+p) uμuν+p gμνT_{\mu\nu} = (\rho + p)\, u_\mu u_\nu + p\, g_{\mu\nu}Tμν​=(ρ+p)uμ​uν​+pgμν​, with ρ\rhoρ energy density, ppp pressure, uμu^\muuμ four-velocity of the fluid (gμνuμuν=−1g_{\mu\nu} u^\mu u^\nu = -1gμν​uμuν=−1).
  • Electromagnetism: Tμν=14π(Fμλ Fνλ−14gμν FρσFρσ)T_{\mu\nu} = \tfrac{1}{4\pi}(F_{\mu\lambda}\, F_\nu{}^\lambda - \tfrac{1}{4} g_{\mu\nu}\, F_{\rho\sigma} F^{\rho\sigma})Tμν​=4π1​(Fμλ​Fν​λ−41​gμν​Fρσ​Fρσ), with FFF the field-strength 222-form.
  • Scalar field: Tμν=∇μϕ ∇νϕ−12 gμν (∇ϕ)2−gμν V(ϕ)T_{\mu\nu} = \nabla_\mu \phi\, \nabla_\nu \phi - \tfrac{1}{2}\, g_{\mu\nu}\, (\nabla \phi)^2 - g_{\mu\nu}\, V(\phi)Tμν​=∇μ​ϕ∇ν​ϕ−21​gμν​(∇ϕ)2−gμν​V(ϕ).

In all three, ∇μTμν=0\nabla^\mu T_{\mu\nu} = 0∇μTμν​=0 follows from the matter field equations — energy conservation is consistent with the geometry, by design.

Geometric content, briefly

A few phrases for the geometric meaning:

  • The Ricci tensor RμνR_{\mu\nu}Rμν​ measures how the volume of a small ball changes as it’s parallel-transported; positive Ricci → focusing, negative → defocusing.
  • The Weyl tensor measures tidal distortion at fixed volume — the trace-free shearing component of curvature.
  • A vacuum solution has all curvature in the Weyl tensor; the Ricci tensor (and hence volumes) is locally flat, but tidal effects (and hence relative geodesic motion) remain.

The single most important consequence — the geodesic equation as the equation of motion for free particles — is what makes the Einstein equations a theory of gravity: matter tells spacetime how to curve, and spacetime tells matter how to move.
