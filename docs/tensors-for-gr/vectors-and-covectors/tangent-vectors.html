Tangent vectors

Matthew Litwin 2026-05-21

A tangent vector at a point ppp of a manifold MMM is an arrow at ppp — a velocity that a curve through ppp could have. The tangent space TpMT_p MTp​M is the nnn-dimensional vector space of all such arrows.

Two views

Embedded view. If M⊆RNM \subseteq \mathbb{R}^NM⊆RN is a smooth submanifold and γ:(−ε,ε)→M\gamma: (-\varepsilon, \varepsilon) \to Mγ:(−ε,ε)→M is a smooth curve with γ(0)=p\gamma(0) = pγ(0)=p, the tangent vector is the ordinary derivative

v=γ′(0)∈RN.v = \gamma'(0) \in \mathbb{R}^N. v=γ′(0)∈RN.

This automatically lies in the affine tangent plane to MMM at ppp. Different curves with the same derivative at 000 give the same tangent vector; TpMT_p MTp​M is the linear span of all such γ′(0)\gamma'(0)γ′(0).

Abstract view. Without an embedding, a tangent vector is a at ppp: an R\mathbb{R}R-linear map v:C∞(M)→Rv: C^\infty(M) \to \mathbb{R}v:C∞(M)→R satisfying the Leibniz rule

v(fg)=f(p) v(g)+g(p) v(f).v(fg) = f(p)\, v(g) + g(p)\, v(f). v(fg)=f(p)v(g)+g(p)v(f).

Equivalently, an equivalence class of curves through ppp with the same first-order behavior in any chart. (See the manifolds book for the full three-definitions taxonomy.)

The two views agree wherever both apply: a curve γ\gammaγ in the embedded picture acts on f∈C∞(M)f \in C^\infty(M)f∈C∞(M) by v(f)=(f∘γ)′(0)v(f) = (f \circ \gamma)'(0)v(f)=(f∘γ)′(0), recovering the derivation.

Derivation

For a commutative algebra AAA over R\mathbb{R}R (e.g., A=C∞(M)A = C^\infty(M)A=C∞(M)), a derivation at p∈Mp \in Mp∈M is an R\mathbb{R}R-linear map D:A→RD: A \to \mathbb{R}D:A→R satisfying the Leibniz rule

D(fg)=f(p) D(g)+g(p) D(f).D(fg) = f(p)\, D(g) + g(p)\, D(f). D(fg)=f(p)D(g)+g(p)D(f).

Every derivation at ppp is determined by its values on a coordinate system around ppp — D(xμ)D(x^\mu)D(xμ) for the chart x=(x1,…,xn)x = (x^1, \ldots, x^n)x=(x1,…,xn) — and the space of derivations is an nnn-dimensional real vector space, isomorphic to TpMT_p MTp​M.

The derivation viewpoint generalizes: a derivation of AAA (with values in AAA) is a vector field; the Lie bracket of vector fields is the commutator of derivations.
