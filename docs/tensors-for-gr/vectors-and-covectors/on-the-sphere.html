The two-sphere

Spherical-coordinate chart

Take the standard parametrization

Φ(θ,φ)=(sin⁡θcos⁡φ,  sin⁡θsin⁡φ,  cos⁡θ),\Phi(\theta, \varphi) = (\sin\theta \cos\varphi, \; \sin\theta \sin\varphi, \; \cos\theta), Φ(θ,φ)=(sinθcosφ,sinθsinφ,cosθ),

This chart misses the two poles (where φ\varphiφ is undefined) and the seam at φ=0\varphi = 0φ=0; a second chart, e.g. rotated 90∘90^\circ90∘ in ZZZ, covers what this one misses.

The tangent basis vectors at p=Φ(θ,φ)p = \Phi(\theta, \varphi)p=Φ(θ,φ), embedded view, are the partial derivatives of Φ\PhiΦ:

∂θ=(cos⁡θcos⁡φ,  cos⁡θsin⁡φ,  −sin⁡θ),∂φ=(−sin⁡θsin⁡φ,  sin⁡θcos⁡φ,  0).\begin{aligned} \partial_\theta &= (\cos\theta \cos\varphi, \; \cos\theta \sin\varphi, \; -\sin\theta), \\ \partial_\varphi &= (-\sin\theta \sin\varphi, \; \sin\theta \cos\varphi, \; 0). \end{aligned}∂θ​∂φ​​=(cosθcosφ,cosθsinφ,−sinθ),=(−sinθsinφ,sinθcosφ,0).​

These are vectors in R3\mathbb{R}^3R3, automatically tangent to the sphere at ppp. Their lengths are ∣∂θ∣=1|\partial_\theta| = 1∣∂θ​∣=1 and ∣∂φ∣=sin⁡θ|\partial_\varphi| = \sin\theta∣∂φ​∣=sinθ — not unit vectors. Stereographic chart

A second chart covers the missing seam. Stereographic projection from the south pole gives

ψS(p)=(x,y)=(X1+Z,  Y1+Z),\psi_S(p) = (x, y) = \left( \frac{X}{1 + Z}, \; \frac{Y}{1 + Z} \right), ψS​(p)=(x,y)=(1+ZX​,1+ZY​),

with ΦS−1(x,y)=(2x,2y,1−x2−y2)/(1+x2+y2)\Phi_S^{-1}(x, y) = (2x, 2y, 1 - x^2 - y^2) / (1 + x^2 + y^2)ΦS−1​(x,y)=(2x,2y,1−x2−y2)/(1+x2+y2). Components transform

A vector field expressed in the spherical chart, say V=∂φV = \partial_\varphiV=∂φ​ (rotation around the ZZZ-axis), has stereographic components determined by the Jacobian:

V′x=∂x∂θ Vθ+∂x∂φ Vφ,V′y=∂y∂θ Vθ+∂y∂φ Vφ.V'^{x} = \frac{\partial x}{\partial \theta}\, V^\theta + \frac{\partial x}{\partial \varphi}\, V^\varphi, \qquad V'^{y} = \frac{\partial y}{\partial \theta}\, V^\theta + \frac{\partial y}{\partial \varphi}\, V^\varphi. V′x=∂θ∂x​Vθ+∂φ∂x​Vφ,V′y=∂θ∂y​Vθ+∂φ∂y​Vφ.

With Vθ=0,Vφ=1V^\theta = 0, V^\varphi = 1Vθ=0,Vφ=1, this evaluates (using x=sin⁡θcos⁡φ/(1+cos⁡θ)x = \sin\theta\cos\varphi / (1+\cos\theta)x=sinθcosφ/(1+cosθ), y=sin⁡θsin⁡φ/(1+cos⁡θ)y = \sin\theta\sin\varphi / (1+\cos\theta)y=sinθsinφ/(1+cosθ)) to

V′x=−y,V′y=x,V'^{x} = -y, \qquad V'^{y} = x, V′x=−y,V′y=x,

the familiar rotation field in the plane. The same vector field has different component functions in the two charts — both are correct, and both transform into each other via the rule from the tangent-vectors page.

Cotangent at a point

In the spherical chart, the dual basis to {∂θ,∂φ}\{\partial_\theta, \partial_\varphi\}{∂θ​,∂φ​} is {dθ,dφ}\{d\theta, d\varphi\}{dθ,dφ}, with dθ(∂θ)=1d\theta(\partial_\theta) = 1dθ(∂θ​)=1, dθ(∂φ)=0d\theta(\partial_\varphi) = 0dθ(∂φ​)=0, and so on. A covector ω=ωθ dθ+ωφ dφ\omega = \omega_\theta\, d\theta + \omega_\varphi\, d\varphiω=ωθ​dθ+ωφ​dφ pairs with v=vθ ∂θ+vφ ∂φv = v^\theta\, \partial_\theta + v^\varphi\, \partial_\varphiv=vθ∂θ​+vφ∂φ​ as

The arithmetic is identical to flat space; what’s coordinate-dependent is the basis being paired, not the pairing itself.

A natural covector field is d(cos⁡θ)=−sin⁡θ dθd(\cos\theta) = -\sin\theta\, d\thetad(cosθ)=−sinθdθ — the differential of the ZZZ-coordinate function. It pairs with ∂φ\partial_\varphi∂φ​ to give zero (the ZZZ-coordinate is rotation-invariant); with ∂θ\partial_\theta∂θ​ to give −sin⁡θ-\sin\theta−sinθ (the rate of change of ZZZ as θ\thetaθ increases).
