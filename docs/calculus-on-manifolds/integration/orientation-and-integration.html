Orientation and integration

Matthew Litwin 2026-05-18

An orientation on an nnn-manifold MMM is a smooth, nowhere-vanishing nnn-form Ω∈Ωn(M)\Omega \in \Omega^n(M)Ω∈Ωn(M), taken up to multiplication by an everywhere-positive smooth function. Two such forms give the same orientation iff their ratio is positive everywhere on MMM.

A manifold that admits an orientation is orientable. Examples: Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn, SnS^nSn, TnT^nTn, every Lie group, every complex manifold. Non-examples: the Möbius strip, the Klein bottle, RP2k\mathbb{RP}^{2k}RP2k.

Equivalently, an orientation is a consistent choice of “right-handed basis” at each tangent space: a connected component of the bundle of frames.

Oriented chart. A chart (U,φ)(U, \varphi)(U,φ) is positively oriented if dx1∧⋯∧dxndx^1 \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^ndx1∧⋯∧dxn agrees with the orientation of MMM on UUU.

Integration of nnn-forms. For a positively oriented chart (U,φ)(U, \varphi)(U,φ) and a compactly supported nnn-form

ω=f dx1∧⋯∧dxn∈Ωcn(U),\omega = f\, dx^1 \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^n \in \Omega^n_c(U), ω=fdx1∧⋯∧dxn∈Ωcn​(U),

define

∫Mω:=∫φ(U)(f∘φ−1) dx1⋯dxn,\int_M \omega := \int_{\varphi(U)} (f \circ \varphi^{-1})\, dx^1 \cdots dx^n, ∫M​ω:=∫φ(U)​(f∘φ−1)dx1⋯dxn,

the right side being an ordinary Riemann/Lebesgue integral on Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn.

For a general ω∈Ωcn(M)\omega \in \Omega^n_c(M)ω∈Ωcn​(M), choose a partition of unity {ρα}\{\rho_\alpha\}{ρα​} subordinate to a positively oriented atlas {(Uα,φα)}\{(U_\alpha, \varphi_\alpha)\}{(Uα​,φα​)} and set

∫Mω:=∑α∫Uαρα ω.\int_M \omega := \sum_\alpha \int_{U_\alpha} \rho_\alpha \, \omega. ∫M​ω:=α∑​∫Uα​​ρα​ω.

Independence of the choice of partition and atlas is exactly the change-of-variables formula in Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn (with Jacobian sign tracked by the orientation).

Change of variables on manifolds. For an orientation-preserving diffeomorphism F:N→MF: N \to MF:N→M,

∫NF∗ω=∫Mω.\int_N F^* \omega = \int_M \omega. ∫N​F∗ω=∫M​ω.

Integration of forms is reparametrization-invariant by construction — that’s the whole motivation for using forms instead of functions.
