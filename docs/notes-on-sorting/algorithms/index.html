Algorithms

Matthew Litwin 2026-05-17

Two classic algorithms, with the trade-offs visible side by side. Both reach the O(nlog⁡n)O(n \log n)O(nlogn) comparison-sort lower bound in their preferred sense, but they differ on stability, memory, and worst case.

The shortest summary you can get away with: quicksort if you want speed on average and don’t need stability, merge sort if you want a guarantee or you need stability.
