Noether's theorem

Matthew Litwin 2026-05-18

Each continuous symmetry of the action gives a quantity conserved along solutions. This is Noether’s theorem (1918), and it’s the structural reason energy, momentum, and angular momentum show up everywhere they do — they’re the conserved quantities attached to the symmetries (time translation, spatial translation, rotation) of the laws of physics.
