Hamiltonian mechanics

Matthew Litwin 2026-05-18

The Hamiltonian formulation trades the second-order Euler–Lagrange equations on configuration space for a first-order system on phase space, via a Legendre transform. The result has a geometric structure — the Poisson bracket — that is the heart of classical mechanics and the bridge to quantum mechanics, statistical mechanics, and symplectic geometry.
