k-forms and the wedge product

Matthew Litwin 2026-06-23

A kkk-form at ppp is an alternating multilinear map

ωp:TpM×⋯×TpM⏟k copies→R\omega_p: \underbrace{T_p M \times \cdots \times T_p M}_{k \text{ copies}} \to \mathbb{R} ωp​:k copiesTp​M×⋯×Tp​M​​→R

— multilinear meaning linear in each argument with the others fixed, alternating meaning it changes sign under any swap of two arguments (equivalently, totally antisymmetric — the term used for the same property in the tensor chapter).

The space of kkk-forms at ppp is ΛkTp∗M\Lambda^k T^*_p MΛkTp∗​M, of dimension (nk)\binom{n}{k}(kn​). By convention Λ0=R\Lambda^0 = \mathbb{R}Λ0=R (numbers), Λ1=Tp∗M\Lambda^1 = T^*_p MΛ1=Tp∗​M (covectors). Λk=0\Lambda^k = 0Λk=0 for k>nk > nk>n.

A differential kkk-form on MMM is a smooth section of ΛkT∗M\Lambda^k T^* MΛkT∗M. The space of such is Ωk(M)\Omega^k(M)Ωk(M). So Ω0(M)=C∞(M)\Omega^0(M) = C^\infty(M)Ω0(M)=C∞(M) and Ω1(M)\Omega^1(M)Ω1(M) is 1-forms.

Coordinate expression. This book uses the 1k!\tfrac{1}{k!}k!1​ throughout:

ω=1k! ωi1⋯ik dxi1∧⋯∧dxik,\omega = \frac{1}{k!}\, \omega_{i_1 \cdots i_k}\, dx^{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^{i_k}, ω=k!1​ωi1​⋯ik​​dxi1​∧⋯∧dxik​,

with ωi1⋯ik∈C∞(U)\omega_{i_1 \cdots i_k} \in C^\infty(U)ωi1​⋯ik​​∈C∞(U) totally antisymmetric and the sum running over all index tuples. (Equivalently, restrict to strictly increasing tuples i1<⋯<iki_1 < \cdots < i_ki1​<⋯<ik​ and drop the 1k!\tfrac{1}{k!}k!1​; the component array on ordered tuples is the same.)

Wedge product. The wedge ∧:Ωk(M)×Ωℓ(M)→Ωk+ℓ(M)\wedge: \Omega^k(M) \times \Omega^\ell(M) \to \Omega^{k+\ell}(M)∧:Ωk(M)×Ωℓ(M)→Ωk+ℓ(M) is the alternating tensor product. The basis kkk-form is the antisymmetrized tensor product of coordinate differentials,

dxμ1∧⋯∧dxμk:=∑σ∈Sksgn(σ)  dxμσ(1)⊗⋯⊗dxμσ(k),dx^{\mu_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^{\mu_k} := \sum_{\sigma \in S_k} \mathrm{sgn}(\sigma)\; dx^{\mu_{\sigma(1)}} \otimes \cdots \otimes dx^{\mu_{\sigma(k)}}, dxμ1​∧⋯∧dxμk​:=σ∈Sk​∑​sgn(σ)dxμσ(1)​⊗⋯⊗dxμσ(k)​,

and wedging concatenates factors:

(dxi1∧⋯∧dxik)∧(dxj1∧⋯∧dxjℓ)=dxi1∧⋯∧dxik∧dxj1∧⋯∧dxjℓ.(dx^{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^{i_k}) \wedge (dx^{j_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^{j_\ell}) = dx^{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^{i_k} \wedge dx^{j_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^{j_\ell}. (dxi1​∧⋯∧dxik​)∧(dxj1​∧⋯∧dxjℓ​)=dxi1​∧⋯∧dxik​∧dxj1​∧⋯∧dxjℓ​.

Properties:

  • Associative: (ω∧η)∧ζ=ω∧(η∧ζ)(\omega \wedge \eta) \wedge \zeta = \omega \wedge (\eta \wedge \zeta)(ω∧η)∧ζ=ω∧(η∧ζ).
  • Graded-commutative: ω∧η=(−1)kℓ η∧ω\omega \wedge \eta = (-1)^{k\ell}\, \eta \wedge \omegaω∧η=(−1)kℓη∧ω for ω∈Ωk\omega \in \Omega^kω∈Ωk, η∈Ωℓ\eta \in \Omega^\ellη∈Ωℓ.
  • Bilinear over R\mathbb{R}R and C∞(M)C^\infty(M)C∞(M).
  • dxi∧dxi=0dx^i \wedge dx^i = 0dxi∧dxi=0; any wedge with repeated factors vanishes.

The total exterior algebra is Ω∗(M):=⨁k=0nΩk(M)\Omega^*(M) := \bigoplus_{k=0}^n \Omega^k(M)Ω∗(M):=⨁k=0n​Ωk(M), a graded-commutative C∞(M)C^\infty(M)C∞(M)-algebra.

Wedge convention

This book writes a kkk-form in the 1/k!1/k!1/k! convention:

ω=1k! ωμ1⋯μk dxμ1∧⋯∧dxμk,\omega = \frac{1}{k!}\, \omega_{\mu_1 \cdots \mu_k}\, dx^{\mu_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^{\mu_k}, ω=k!1​ωμ1​⋯μk​​dxμ1​∧⋯∧dxμk​,

with ωμ1⋯μk\omega_{\mu_1 \cdots \mu_k}ωμ1​⋯μk​​ totally antisymmetric and the sum over all index tuples. The common alternative — the strictly-increasing-index convention —

ω=∑μ1<⋯<μkωμ1⋯μk dxμ1∧⋯∧dxμk\omega = \sum_{\mu_1 < \cdots < \mu_k} \omega_{\mu_1 \cdots \mu_k}\, dx^{\mu_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^{\mu_k} ω=μ1​<⋯<μk​∑​ωμ1​⋯μk​​dxμ1​∧⋯∧dxμk​

carries no combinatorial prefactor and sums only over ordered tuples. The two give the same component array on ordered tuples (e.g. ωθφ=sin⁡θ\omega_{\theta\varphi} = \sin\thetaωθφ​=sinθ for the sphere’s area form either way); they differ only in whether the basis is reduced to ordered tuples or the components are antisymmetrized and divided by k!k!k!. Watch for the factor when comparing formulas across textbooks.
