General relativity

Matthew Litwin 2026-06-23

General relativity is the geometric theory of gravity built on the tensor calculus of the previous four sections. Spacetime is a Lorentzian 444-manifold; matter and energy are described by a stress–energy tensor TμνT_{\mu\nu}Tμν​; the metric is determined by the Einstein equations, which equate the Einstein tensor (geometry) to a constant times TμνT_{\mu\nu}Tμν​ (matter content).

Three pages: the Einstein equations and their geometric content; the Schwarzschild solution as the torsion-free worked example; Einstein–Cartan gravity, where torsion is allowed and couples to spin.

This is “tensors for GR”, not “GR.” The pages set up the field equations and compute one example each — not a survey of GR phenomena. Standard textbooks pick up from here.
