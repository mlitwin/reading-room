Tangent space

Matthew Litwin 2026-06-23

The tangent space at ppp, denoted TpMT_p MTp​M, is the nnn-dimensional vector space of “directions” at ppp — the velocities a curve through ppp could have.

Three equivalent definitions

  1. Curves modulo first-order tangency. Equivalence classes of smooth curves γ:(−ε,ε)→M\gamma: (-\varepsilon, \varepsilon) \to Mγ:(−ε,ε)→M with γ(0)=p\gamma(0) = pγ(0)=p under γ1∼γ2  ⟺  (φ∘γ1)′(0)=(φ∘γ2)′(0)\gamma_1 \sim \gamma_2 \iff (\varphi \circ \gamma_1)'(0) = (\varphi \circ \gamma_2)'(0)γ1​∼γ2​⟺(φ∘γ1​)′(0)=(φ∘γ2​)′(0) for some (equivalently, any) chart.

  2. Derivations at ppp. Linear maps v:C∞(M)→Rv: C^\infty(M) \to \mathbb{R}v:C∞(M)→R satisfying Leibniz:

v(fg)=f(p) v(g)+g(p) v(f).v(fg) = f(p)\, v(g) + g(p)\, v(f). v(fg)=f(p)v(g)+g(p)v(f).

  1. Coordinate nnn-tuples that transform. Tuples (v1,…,vn)∈Rn(v^1, \ldots, v^n) \in \mathbb{R}^n(v1,…,vn)∈Rn assigned to each chart, related across charts by the Jacobian of the transition map (below).

The three define the same space; which is most convenient depends on the construction at hand.

Tangent bundle

TM:=⨆pTpMTM := \bigsqcup_p T_p MTM:=⨆p​Tp​M is itself a smooth manifold of dimension 2n2n2n, with projection π:TM→M\pi: TM \to Mπ:TM→M and natural smooth structure coming from the charts on MMM.

Embedded manifold

A smooth submanifold of RN\mathbb{R}^NRN is a subset M⊆RNM \subseteq \mathbb{R}^NM⊆RN that locally looks like the graph of a smooth function: around every p∈Mp \in Mp∈M there is an open U⊆RNU \subseteq \mathbb{R}^NU⊆RN, an open V⊆RnV \subseteq \mathbb{R}^nV⊆Rn, and a smooth embedding Φ:V→U∩M\Phi: V \to U \cap MΦ:V→U∩M whose differential is everywhere injective. The image Φ(V)\Phi(V)Φ(V) is an open piece of MMM, and the nnn coordinates on VVV are a chart on MMM.

The embedded view of differential geometry takes advantage of the ambient RN\mathbb{R}^NRN: tangent vectors are vectors in RN\mathbb{R}^NRN (sitting in the affine tangent plane to MMM); the metric is the pullback of the Euclidean inner product; integration is over an embedded submanifold of RN\mathbb{R}^NRN. The Whitney embedding theorem says every smooth nnn-manifold can be embedded in R2n\mathbb{R}^{2n}R2n, so the embedded view loses no generality in principle. In practice it can be inconvenient — Lorentzian spacetimes do not embed isometrically in any flat ambient — which is why the abstract view is also needed.
