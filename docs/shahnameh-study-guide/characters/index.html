Cast of Characters

Matthew Litwin 2026-05-17

The Shahnameh’s family lines are not optional reading. Many of the poem’s most affecting moments — Sohrab killed by his own father Rostam without either of them knowing it; Siyavash’s exile and murder by his father-in-law Afrasiyab; the long blood feud between Iran and Turan over Iraj’s killing — are intelligible only if you can keep the relations straight.

This section sketches the four most important groups.

  1. The primordial kings (Pishdadi) — Kayumars, Hushang, Tahmures, Jamshid, Zahhak, Fereydun, and the brothers who split the world.
  2. The heroes of Sistan — Sam, Zal, Rostam, Sohrab. The line that holds Iran together when its kings can’t.
  3. The Kayanian shahs — Kay Qobad, Kay Kavus, Siyavash, Kay Khosrow, Lohrasp, Goshtasp, Esfandiyar.
  4. The Turanians — Afrasiyab and the long eastern enemy.

The lists below are deliberately partial — the Shahnameh has hundreds of named characters. These are the people whose names you’ll need at the top of your memory.
