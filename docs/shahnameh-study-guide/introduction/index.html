Introduction and Overview

Matthew Litwin 2026-05-17

The Shahnameh sits between three traditions — the deep cultural memory of pre-Islamic Iran, the religion of Zarathustra that shaped that memory for more than a thousand years, and the Islam that displaced it as the state religion in Ferdowsi’s own century. To read the poem on its own terms, you need to know enough about each to hear what the poem is actually saying.

The three short essays in this section are not surveys; they’re orientation. You don’t need to know everything about Iran, Zoroastrianism, or early Islamic Iran to read the Shahnameh, but you do need to know that the poem isn’t speaking out of any one of them in isolation.
