---
title: The metric
---

A bare manifold doesn't have lengths, angles, or volumes. The **metric tensor** is the piece of extra structure that introduces all three. In general relativity, the metric is the gravitational field: its values at each point encode the geometry of spacetime, and Einstein's equations are conditions the metric must satisfy.

Three pages: the metric as a $(0, 2)$-tensor and its signature; raising and lowering indices; the round metric on $S^2$ in coordinates.
