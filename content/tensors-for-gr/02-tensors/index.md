title: Tensors
---

A tensor is a multilinear gadget that eats some vectors and some covectors and returns a real number. The same object has two names: physicists call it an $(r, s)$-tensor with $r$ upper and $s$ lower indices; mathematicians call it an element of $T_p M^{\otimes r} \otimes T^*_p M^{\otimes s}$. They are identical.

Four pages: the multilinear-map definition and tensor product; how indices and the transformation rule encode it; the side-by-side dictionary between index notation, coordinate-free notation, and (anti-symmetric) $k$-forms; tensors and forms on $S^2$ worked out in components.
