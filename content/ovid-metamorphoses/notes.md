Short definitions of the grammatical terms used by the card popovers throughout this book. Pop one up by clicking the term inside a card; read it as a glossary in its own right by scrolling here.

## Supine

A defective verbal noun in `-um` (or `-u`), used in a narrow set of constructions: with verbs of motion to express purpose (*venit spectatum*, "he came to see"), and as the [fourth principal part](note:fourth-principal-part) of a verb's dictionary entry — the form that gives the stem for the [perfect passive participle](note:perfect-passive-participle) and the future active participle. Allen & Greenough: [§508](ag:508).

## Gender

The three Latin grammatical genders are [masculine](note:masculine), [feminine](note:feminine), and [neuter](note:neuter). Nouns have a fixed gender; adjectives shift to match the noun they modify. Allen & Greenough: [§29](ag:29).

## Declension

One of five patterns of noun (and adjective) inflection in Latin, identified by the genitive singular ending. 1st declension: `-ae` (feminine, like *forma*). 2nd: `-i` (masculine and neuter). 3rd: `-is` (all three genders). 4th: `-us` (mostly masculine). 5th: `-ei` (mostly feminine). Allen & Greenough: [§36](ag:36).

## Principal parts

The set of four verb forms a dictionary entry gives so the reader can build any inflected form. For a regular verb: [1st](note:first-principal-part) = the lemma's dictionary form (1st-person singular present indicative active, e.g. *amo*); [2nd](note:second-principal-part) = present active infinitive (*amare*); [3rd](note:third-principal-part) = 1st-person singular perfect indicative active (*amavi*); [4th](note:fourth-principal-part) = supine (*amatum*). The stems these give are A&G's three verb stems — Allen & Greenough: [§164](ag:164).

## First principal part

The first slot in a verb's dictionary entry — the 1st-person singular present indicative active (e.g. *amo*, "I love"). This is the form the lemma is filed under, and it gives the present stem from which all present-system forms are built.

## Second principal part

The second slot in a verb's dictionary entry — the present active [infinitive](note:infinitive) (e.g. *amare*, "to love"). It shows which [conjugation](note:declension) the verb belongs to and how the present system inflects.

## Third principal part

The third slot in a verb's dictionary entry — the 1st-person singular perfect indicative active (e.g. *amavi*, "I have loved"). It gives the perfect stem, from which all forms in the [perfect](note:perfect), [pluperfect](note:pluperfect), and future-perfect active are built.

## Fourth principal part

The fourth slot in a verb's dictionary entry — the [supine](note:supine) (e.g. *amatum*). It gives the stem for the [perfect passive participle](note:perfect-passive-participle) and the future active participle.
