---
title: Algorithms
---

Two classic algorithms, with the trade-offs visible side by side. Both reach the $O(n \log n)$ comparison-sort lower bound in their preferred sense, but they differ on stability, memory, and worst case.

The shortest summary you can get away with: **quicksort** if you want speed on average and don't need stability, **merge sort** if you want a guarantee or you need stability.
