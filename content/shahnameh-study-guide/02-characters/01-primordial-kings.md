---
title: The Pishdadi line
---

The **Pishdadi** ("foremost-created") are the first kings of the Shahnameh — its mythical age. Their reigns are measured in centuries, their enemies are demons (*divs*), and their inventions are civilization itself: fire, the smithy, the loom, the calendar.

- **Kayumars** — First man and first king. Lives in the mountains, wears leopard skins. His reign is undone when his only son **Siyamak** is killed by the demon Ahriman's son.
- **Hushang** — Grandson of Kayumars (son of Siyamak). Avenges his father, founds the Pishdadi lineage formally, and discovers fire by striking a stone at a snake.
- **Tahmures** — Son of Hushang. "The Binder of Demons" — captures the divs and forces them to teach humans writing in thirty languages.
- **Jamshid** — Son of Tahmures. The greatest of the Pishdadi: organizes society into four estates, invents weaving and metallurgy, founds the festival of **Nowruz**. Reigns seven hundred years. Eventually claims divinity, loses his *farr*, is overthrown.
- **Zahhak** — Arab prince, son of Mirdas, seduced into evil by Ahriman, who kisses his shoulders; from the kisses grow two black serpents that demand to be fed the brains of young men daily. Reigns a thousand years. The poem's portrait of him is a study in tyranny.
- **Fereydun** — Descendant of Jamshid through a hidden line. As a baby, hidden from Zahhak's slaughter and raised on the milk of the cow Birmaye. As a man, leads the revolt that overthrows Zahhak. Reigns five hundred years; divides the world among his three sons:
  - **Salm** — the eldest, given **Rum** (the West).
  - **Tur** — the middle son, given **Turan** (the East).
  - **Iraj** — the youngest, given **Iran**.
- **Iraj** is murdered by his envious brothers — the original sin of the poem. His grandson **Manuchehr** avenges him by killing Salm and Tur, and the war between Iran and Turan never ends thereafter.

After Manuchehr come a series of less individualized kings (Nowzar, Zaav, Garshasp). The line is failing, and the next age belongs to a different lineage entirely.
