---
title: The heroes of Sistan
---

The Shahnameh's central hero is not a king. **Rostam** of Sistan — a vassal of the Kayanian shahs, born to a princely line in southeastern Iran — overshadows every monarch he serves, and the poem returns to his family across generations.

- **Garshasp** — A legendary hero in Avestan tradition, named as a distant ancestor; minor in the Shahnameh itself.
- **Nariman** — Hero of an earlier generation.
- **Sam** — Son of Nariman. Champion of King Manuchehr.
- **Zal** — Son of Sam. Born albino (white hair, pale skin); Sam exposes him on a mountain, but the **Simurgh**, a mythical bird, raises him. Sam later repents; Zal is restored as the heir of Sistan.
- **Rudabeh** — Princess of Kabul, daughter of King **Mehrab** (who is a descendant of Zahhak). Falls in love with Zal; their courtship is one of the poem's lyric high points; they marry against political resistance on both sides.
- **Rostam** — Their son. Delivered by caesarean section (the Simurgh advises Zal how). Strongest man in the world from infancy. His horse **Rakhsh** is his match in spirit. He performs the **Seven Labors** (*haft khwan*), kills the **White Demon**, and serves several Kayanian shahs across what the poem treats as several centuries of service.
- **Tahmineh** — Princess of Samangan. Rostam meets her on one of his journeys, marries her one night, leaves her with a token. Their son is **Sohrab**.
- **Sohrab** — Grows up not knowing his father. Leads a Turanian expedition west, looking for Rostam; meets him in single combat without either recognizing the other. Rostam kills him. The discovery of who he has killed is the poem's most-cited tragedy.
- **Faramarz** — Rostam's son by another union. Continues the line.
- **Shaghad** — Rostam's half-brother (by a different mother). Eventually betrays Rostam in old age, leading him to his death by pitfall.

The Sistani line is the moral anchor of the poem. Kings come and go, often badly; the family of Sam holds the country together.
